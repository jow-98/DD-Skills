## AlphaSights Briefing — Bislab (May 2025)

**Subject:** Expert Calls Bislab x 42CAP x AlphaSights

---

Hi Elon,

I hope you are doing well. We would be interested in talking with 5-6 experts as part of our due diligence process on Bislab. The timeline to schedule all of the calls is until 28 May.

Attached to this email is Bislab's Product deck. It should give you some context on what they do. Feel free to share it with the experts prior to the call so that they can prepare themselves.

Also, you can share our name, as well as the name of the company prior to the call. The expert should be aware that a VC is consulting them to validate Bislab's value proposition as a potential customer.

In Short: Bislab is building a new kind of credit bureau from the ground up — modern, API-first, and trusted by leading Nordic enterprises. Providing highly accurate credit scoring for both individuals and businesses. Instead of relying solely on outdated models or limited data, their system pulls from a wide array of public and private sources (e.g. national company registries and data brokers) to generate more precise insights. Clients can use this to assess creditworthiness, set appropriate credit limits, and monitor corporate entities in real time. Whether it's onboarding new customers or running ongoing due diligence, their tools are built for seamless integration into existing workflows.

The company is positioned to replace national legacy providers (such as SCHUFA, Equifax, TransUnion, Experian, Dun & Bradstreet, or CRIF) and enables 65% fewer credit rejections without increasing risk. They are already working with customers in financial services, utilities, and insurance, Telco and Debt Collection.

The following types of experts are of interest:
Overall, we would like to set up 4-5 expert calls with potential customers / users of Bislab. We are looking for:
- Potential Job titles of the expert are: Chief Risk Officer, Head of Credit, Risk Manager, Compliance Officer, Credit Risk Analyst.
- Industries: Financial services, lending, insurance, utilities, debt collection, rental, large-scale retail. For example, their current client list includes intrum, kravia, tietoevry, fremtind, or Telavox. Please extend the research and look for similar companies offering any type of lending, insurance, or telecom service online such as Klarna, Revolut, Funding Circle, Monzo, N26, Clark etc.
- Key priorities:
  - Reducing credit risk and NPLs
  - Improving onboarding speed and automation
  - Detecting fraud, integrating diverse data sources
  - Ensuring compliance with national regulations
- We would like to understand how Bislab's approach differentiates from existing credit bureau solutions such as Equifax, SCHUFA, Experian, or Dun & Bradstreet and if there is a willingness to change (i.e. rip and replace) their existing credit risk models. So it would be beneficial if the expert is familiar with any of the solutions named above.

In addition, we'd also like to run 1-2 calls with technical experts from credit bureaus or similar backgrounds — ideally people who:
- Have worked hands-on with scoring models (e.g. using graph theory, time-series models, or proprietary fraud logic)
- Can assess Bislab's claim to "build the best risk models via software" and its potential to challenge traditional players
- Help us challenge the company's tech narrative

Format of the call:
- 35 minute call between the experts and Bislab — 10-15 minute product demo and 15-25 minutes Q&A. This is followed by 25-30 minutes feedback with the experts and us.
- As there will be a product demo, the expert has to sit in front of a laptop and should not dial in via phone. Please communicate this very clearly prior to the call. We had some issues here in the past. If the expert indicates that he cannot make it in front of a laptop, we prefer to reschedule the call. This is a technical product and requires a visual demonstration.
- If the founding team has problems kicking-off the conversation, tell the experts that they should specifically ask for a product demo or some slides on the product.
- Expert calls should take place in the coming days, until 28 May the latest. I will ask the founder of Bislab for his availability and send you over the times asap.
- The expert does not have to take the lead for the first 10-15 minutes. I will talk to Bislab prior to the call and tell them explicitly that they should set the context and have a product demo prepared.
- After the product demo, the experts should take over and lead the conversation to provide us with answers to the questions below.

The following discussion topics are of interest to us:
- How convincing is Bislab's performance advantage vs. legacy bureaus?
- What's the role of data moats and network effects in this space?
- How hard is it to replace an incumbent in this market? How strong is the data moat of the incumbent credit bureaus?
- How does Bislab overcome the "trust gap" in Go-To-Market when replacing decades-old providers?
- Do you see this as a Nordic-only platform, or could it credibly scale across Europe?
- Could this become a real alternative to Experian, Schufa, or Bisnode, or is there regulation that prevents new businesses from entering the market?

Let me know if you want to jump on a call to discuss details or if you have any questions on the company and what they are doing in detail.

Best,
Johannes

## Key Formatting Rules (derived from real emails)

### Subject lines
- AlphaSights: `Expert Calls [Company Name] x 42CAP x AlphaSights`
- Founders: `[Company Name] Expert Calls with 42CAP and AlphaSights`

### AlphaSights salutation
Always `Hi Elon,` — the AlphaSights contact is Elon.

### Founder salutation
Use first names: `Hi [Name],` for one founder; `Hi [Name1], hi [Name2],` for multiple (lowercase "hi" after the comma).

### Founder intro
Always identify yourself: "This is Johannes from 42CAP — I'm supporting [Partner1] and [Partner2] in the due diligence process." Pull the responsible partners from Affinity or Granola meeting history.

### Deck note (AlphaSights)
The deck CAN be shared with experts. Phrase: "Feel free to share it with the experts prior to the call so that they can prepare themselves." Only add the "do not share" note if the founder specifically requests it.

### Expert profile structure (AlphaSights)
Three subsections within the expert type block:
1. Potential job titles
2. Industries (with named example companies from the ICP)
3. Key priorities (bulleted pain points)
Optionally add a second block for 1-2 technical/domain experts if relevant.

### Discussion topics (AlphaSights)
Framed as direct questions, not topic headings. Derived from WYHTBI theses. Typically 5-7 questions.

### Business impact tip (Founder)
Always include the "small tip" paragraph — it coaches founders to lead with business value, not technical performance.

### Language
- AlphaSights: always English
- Founders: English (default) or German — ask if not specified
- German: informal "du/ihr" throughout, never "Sie"; salutation `Hi [Name], hi [Name2],` (lowercase second "hi")
